The Reggy++ chatbot serves as a practical demonstration of rule-based Natural Language Understanding. After running multiple test cases with varying date formats, names, and mood expressions, I have observed several key insights regarding its naturalness, strengths, and inherent technical flaws.

Naturalness and User Experience In terms of naturalness, Reggy++ feels significantly more advanced than a basic "if-else" keyword matcher, but it remains far from a truly conversational AI. The interaction feels "semi-natural" when it correctly extracts a surname or calculates age from a text-based date like "25 Aug 04." However, the conversation is strictly one-way. The chatbot follows a rigid script and cannot handle follow-up questions or changes in topic. The most "human-like" moment occurs during mood detection; by using regex quantifiers to handle typos like "sssad" or "haapy," the bot mimics a level of tolerance that makes the user feel understood despite minor errors.

Strengths of Regex-based Interaction From a technical standpoint, the primary strength of this system is its extreme efficiency. Because it relies solely on the re module and basic string processing, it executes instantly without requiring any external machine learning libraries or heavy computational resources. It is highly effective for "Slot Filling" tasksâ€”extracting structured data from unstructured text. For instance, the ability to recognize diverse date formats (numeric vs. text) using multiple patterns shows that regex is a powerful tool for standardized data extraction where the variations are predictable.

Limitations and Failure Cases The limitations of a regex-based system become glaringly obvious when faced with semantic complexity. The most notable failure in my tests was the sentence "i am not happy." Technically, the regex successfully found the pattern h+a+p+y and triggered a positive response. However, it completely failed to understand the "negation" (the word 'not'). This proves that regex identifies tokens but does not understand meaning or context.

Another failure occurred when the birthday was provided in a narrative format ("On a rainy Sunday in 2004"). Since the pattern was looking for specific month/day anchors, it could not parse the intent. These cases highlight that while Reggy++ is fast and precise for rules, it lacks the probabilistic intelligence required for true language "understanding."
